About Me

I'm a technologist working at the intersection of accessibility, AI, and language systems. My work centers on understanding how technology can be designed, implemented, and improved to create more equitable access to information.

What I Do

At Rochester Institute of Technology, I coordinate real-time captioning services while conducting applied research in natural language processing and speech recognition. I've analyzed over 14,000 academic transcripts to develop domain-specific language models and have built production systems ranging from equipment management applications to experimental transcription enhancement tools.

My technical work spans multiple domains: I develop systems in Rust and Python, fine-tune language models on specialized corpora, and implement speech recognition pipelines. But the through-line in all of it is a focus on understanding what people actually need from technology—and then building solutions that deliver on that promise.

I'm particularly interested in the gap between what commercial technology claims to do and what it actually delivers in practice. I've spent considerable time reverse-engineering systems to understand their true capabilities, conducting accuracy analyses on speech recognition platforms, and advocating for evidence-based adoption of accessibility technologies.

This Site

This site serves as a repository for my technical work, research findings, and analysis of accessibility technology implementations. You'll find documentation of systems I've built, corpus analysis results, and writing on the state of real-time transcription and captioning technology.

I believe technology should be evaluated rigorously, implemented thoughtfully, and continuously improved based on actual performance rather than vendor promises.
